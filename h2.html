3 Muslim Philosophers

Sultan Muhammad al-Fateh

Sultan Muhammad al-Fateh is one of the most famous leaders of the Islamic government recorded in world history. He was not only great as a leader but his name as a warlord, known in continental Europe. Hearing his name alone made his enemy tremble even though he had been dead for 10 years. He was the first Islamic government to receive the title given by the West as "Grand Seigneur" which means great leader. Sultan Muhammad al-Fateh was born on 26 Rejab 833 hijrah equivalent to 20 April 1429 AD & grew up in Adirnah. His father, Sultan Murad II was one of the great leaders and famous figures of the Ottoman government. Her mother is said to be a princess of Christian descent.

Sultan Muhammad al-Fateh was appointed leader of the Ottoman Empire in February 1451 after the death of his father. This appointment means that he is the youngest leader ever appointed because he was only 22 years old, he managed to bring excellence in his rule. The strength of Sultan Muhammad al-Fateh can be seen based on the height of his personality. When he was young, he was taught to ride horses, archery and use swords. But spiritual education received serious attention and full encouragement from his father. He has been intensively educated by the foremost scholars among them is al-Sheikh Muhammad bin Ismail al-Kurani. In addition, he studied and learned a lot from the experience of Rasulullah s.a.w. in the war. Even the spirit of heroism & sacrifice of the former Islamic warlords, byk influenced his soul. However, Sultan Muhammad al-Fateh inherited the qualities of courage, valor, patience, and unwavering spirit of his father. In fact, he also inherited extensive knowledge and skills in formulating war strategies.

Apart from his success in the military field and the conquest of territories, Sultan Muhammad al-Fateh also contributed to the spread of Islam to continental Europe. Through his morals & personality, many were attracted & embraced Islam and throughout his reign, he did not forget to develop the physical & spiritual aspects of his people. He built the al-Fath Mosque known as the Blue Mosque. Apart from that, he has also restored the role of Aya Sofia Mosque as a landmark & ​​syiar of Islam in Turkey. Sultan Muhammad al-Fateh died in May 1481 & his body was buried in Constantinople (Istanbul).

MAULANA JALALUDDIN RUMI

Maulana Jalaluddin Rumi was a Sufi figure in Konya, Turkey around the 13th century. His original name was Maulana Jalal Ad-Din Muhammad Balkhi-Rumi. He is a famous figure who has changed the human soul with love, peace and unity around the world, even his work has been recognized by the World Heritage Board (UNESCO). He also has works of poetry compiled in various languages such as Persian, Turkish , Arabic and Greek.

SHEIKH IBN 'ARABI

Sheikh Ibn 'Arabi is a religious scholar & scholar from Andalus who has many advantages of religious sciences including memorizing the Qur'an when he was 9 years old, mastering the science of qira'at, mathematical calculation, geometry, expert in the science of fiqh & kalam, hadith, tasawwuf and had time to study with Imam al-Ghazali ra even while living in Damascus Syria had time to meet with Baha ad-Din Walad, the father of Maulana Jalaluddin Rumi who was a great Sufi figure in Turkey. Sheikh Ibn 'Arabi wrote more than 500 manuscripts of knowledge but until today there are only about 100 who were saved after Andalus fell into the hands of the Spanish Christians. There are many positive views of the eminent scholars such as Imam as-Suyuti ra, Sheikh Ibn 'Atha'illah, As-Sheikh Shafiyuddin ibn Abi al-Manshur, al -Hafizh Ibn Hajar Asqolani was even admired by as-Syahid Sheikh Saed Ramadan al-Bouti (the Great Scholar of Syria) who stated that Ibn Arabi was al-Imam al-Akbar whose name had been tarnished by the Wahhabis who sabotaged in many books attributed to Ibn 'Arabi falsely.
